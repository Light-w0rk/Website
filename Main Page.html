Tanki Online

 Page Hulls

Tanki Online is a massively played multiplayer game that is worldwide. Players compete with each other. Every tank destroyed is considered a kill and grants the player experience points. The game features many battle modes, such as team modes, as well as a ranking system and many ways to combine equipment and customize tanks. Players are able to choose and try out hundreds of different combinations from the different selections of turrets, hulls, and protrection paints, that fit their taste and playing style. Soon, a new version of Tanki Online would be released that is based off of Unity rather than Flash. In fact, the developers have already allowed closed beta testing to the new version, which means the open release date of Tanki X is right in the front door!

Develpement History

Tanki Online was introduced in May 2009 at the Russian Game Develepers Conference. It was awarded the "Best Game without a Publisher" and "Best Technology" awards. Tanki Online uses AlternativaPlatform's game engine- Alternativa 3D 8, based on Flash technology. In two consecutive years, 2014 and 2015, the game won the main prize in the category "People's Choice" at the Runet Prize. In 2014, Tanki Onlie joined the cybersport program on StarLadder. The game was present at Gamescom 2014 and at the Brasil Game Show 2014.

Supplies


There are five different supplies in Tanki Online. They are:

Repair Kit
Restores 200 armor points per second and reduces burning and freezing effects for 2 seconds
Double Armor
Doubles the armor of your tank for 45 seconds
Double Damage
Doubles the damage of your gun for 45 seconds
Speed Boost
Increases speed of your tank by 30% for 45 seconds
Mine
Quietly sneak behind enemy lines to mine their headquarters, cover your friends during retreat, block approaches to your base- all of it is possible thanks to this deadly device.




Battle Modes

There are three different battle modes in Tanki Online. They are:



Deathmatch (DM)

In Deathmatch mode, unlike in team battle modes, everything depends on you. The battle fund grows a lot faster than in CTFs and TDMs, as you don’t need to go to a certain position to find enemies; they are everywhere. On most maps, you can open fire right from your spawn point. Deathmatch is the simplest and most reliable battle mode when it comes to earning crystals. The battle can be limited by either time or number of kills.





Team Deathmatch (TDM)

It’s really easy to play TDM mode. Battle mechanics are very similar to Deathmatch mode. The only difference is that here, players work in teams. The goal is to destroy more tanks than the opposing team. TDM games are limited by either number of kills or time. Every team member is responsible for the result, as the battle’s final score depends on how well and how carefully they play. Also, unlike in Deathmatch mode, players’ spawn points are located at their bases. To start fighting, you need to go to the centre of a map, or even to the opposing team’s base.





Capture the Flag (CTF)

Players are divided into 2 teams. The goal is to take and capture the enemy’s flag, and to prevent the opposing team from capturing yours. If you take the flag and then get killed, you lose it and the flag is dropped where you were killed. However, your teammates can retake it and continue. If the flag is touched by an enemy’s tank, it automatically returns to the flag holder (generally in the enemies' base). The same applies to your team’s flag. To capture the flag, you need to pick up the enemy’s flag and bring it to your flag. For the capture to be successful, your flag needs to be at your base. Victory goes to the team who has captured the most flags by the end of the game, or who reaches the target amount of captures specified in the battle settings. CTF mode is the most popular mode in Tanki Online.





Control Points (CP)

Teams fight for control over certain points on the map. A team gets score-points when the point is under their control and loses score-points when the point is under enemy's control. The more points captured, the more score-points the team gets and the less score-points the team loses. The winner is the team with the most score-points. Team spawn points are close to their bases.








Ranking System

There are a total of 30 ranks in Tanki Online. The higher the rank a player is, the more (modifications of) turrets and hulls they unlock, as well as more advanced and stunning paints. The lowest rank is recruit and the highest rank is Generalissimo. Players ranks when they have earned the required amount of experience points. As shown in the image below, a player has to earn 145 000 experience points to rank from Commander to Generalissimo, while a total amount of 1 400 000 experience points are required to reach the highest rank from the lowest rank.









Garage

This is the place where players can purchase the turrets, hulls, paints, and supplies. All available items and improvements can be purchased for crystals, which is the currency of the game. An average M0 costs a thousand crystals while an average M3 costs 200 000 crystals.









Upgrade System

There are 3 different modifications of every turret and hull. Each modification improves the combat characteristics of a tank and changes its appearance into a more advanced style. The basic modification is M0 (modification 0), and the 3 modifications of it are M1, M2, M3, with modification 3 being the most advanced and modification 1 being the least.
